Dataless Analytics: Train Your Mind Before You Train Your Model

The Premise

Most courses start with a dataset.
This one starts with your brain.

Because real-world analysts don’t always get clean data. They get:

  • Messy questions
  • Half-built dashboards
  • Stakeholders breathing down their necks

This course builds the rare skill most data professionals skip:

Reasoning from first principles — before the data even exists.

Why This Matters

We live in an era of accelerated AI, but the old rule still applies:

Garbage in, garbage out.

LLMs and models are only as smart as the data they’re fed.
The real edge? Crafting good data on purpose.

This course teaches you how to think like a data-generating machine.

What You'll Learn

By the end of this course, you'll know how to:

  • Generate synthetic datasets (tabular + text-based)
  • Reverse-engineer dashboards and models
  • Surface your priors and challenge your assumptions
  • Evaluate whether data maps well to reality
  • Communicate insights like a strategist

How It Works

Instead of starting with a dataset, you’ll be given:

  • A model
  • A dashboard
  • A decision scenario

Your job?

Invent the data that justifies it.
Work backwards from output to input — just like in real life.

Course Phases

Each phase is built to help you think like a strategist and act like a builder.
Expect lessons, videos, and hands-on challenges in Google Colab.

  • Phase 0: Welcome to the War Room
  • Phase 1: Synthetic Data as a Thinking Tool
  • Phase 2: The Arenas (Simulation Challenges)
  • Phase 3: Capstone – Design for Judgment

Who It's For

This course is for:

  • Data scientists & analysts
  • Product managers & ops leads
  • Anyone who’s ever been told “just build the model” and thought:
    there’s got to be a better way

The Real Payoff

This isn’t just about better models. It’s about:

  • Better conversations with stakeholders
  • Smarter collaboration with teammates
  • Confidence in the chaos before data even exists
If you can think clearly before the data shows up, you’ll lead the room when it does.
