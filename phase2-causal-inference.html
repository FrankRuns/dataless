Challenge: Causal Inference Fire Drill

Description

In this challenge, you’ll use synthetic data to prove the treatment effect of a business intervention under constraints. You’ll generate data for a controlled experiment, such as a marketing campaign, and estimate the causal impact on a key outcome like sales.

Video: Causal Inference with Synthetic Data

This video explains how to design synthetic data for causal inference, including setting up treatment and control groups, balancing covariates, and estimating treatment effects.

Explanation

Causal inference requires isolating the effect of a treatment (e.g., a marketing campaign) on an outcome (e.g., sales) while controlling for confounding variables. In this fire drill, you’ll generate synthetic data with treatment and control groups, ensuring balanced covariates like customer age and purchase history. You’ll then estimate the treatment effect under constraints, such as limited sample size or noisy data, using techniques like propensity score matching. The goal is to accurately quantify the campaign’s impact on sales.
